These are the detailed instructions for how to make the teriyaki chicken

To make teriyaki chicken, start by preparing the teriyaki sauce. In a small bowl, combine 1/4 cup of soy sauce, 1/4 cup of mirin (sweet rice wine), and 2 tablespoons of sugar. For extra flavor, you can add 1 teaspoon of grated ginger and 1 clove of minced garlic. Whisk the ingredients together until the sugar dissolves, then set the sauce aside. Next, heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Pat 4 boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts dry with paper towels to help them brown evenly, then add them to the pan. Cook for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is golden brown and nearly cooked through. Once the chicken is browned, pour the prepared teriyaki sauce into the pan. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer, occasionally flipping the chicken to coat it evenly. Cook until the sauce thickens and becomes a glossy glaze, about 5-10 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pan and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing. Serve the teriyaki chicken over steamed rice, and garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions for a delicious finish.
